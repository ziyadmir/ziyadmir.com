AI and Layoffs: The Future of Work in the Age of Automation

Series Overview

AI Careers Future of Work

I build AI systems for a living. At Roblox that means models making moderation decisions that people used to make, at a volume no human team could cover. Which puts me in an awkward spot in the AI-and-jobs conversation: I am both the person building the automation and a person whose own work is being automated underneath him.

This series is my attempt to think through what that means for careers, from the inside rather than from think-piece distance. I'm not interested in whether AI eliminates work in the abstract — that question is too big to be useful. The sharper questions are which parts of the work get cheap to produce, which parts stay expensive to verify, and who gets to move up into deciding what should be built at all.
