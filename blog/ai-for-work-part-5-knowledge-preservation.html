Your organization is suffering from digital amnesia. That critical Slack thread explaining why the architecture decision was made? Gone after 10 days. Why Traditional Solutions Fail

The Documentation Myth

"Just document everything properly!" sounds great until you realize:

  • Timing Problem: Engineers document after the work is done (if at all)
  • Context Loss: By documentation time, critical discussion context is already deleted
  • Staleness: Wiki pages become outdated without the original conversation threads
  • Discovery Issues: Even well-documented decisions are hard to find months later

The Archive Everything Approach

Strategy Problem Result
Auto-archive all Slack TBs of noise, no signal Search becomes impossible
Backup all emails Compliance nightmares Legal blocks access
Never delete wikis Outdated content proliferates Trust in documentation drops
External knowledge base Another tool to maintain Adoption fails

SaveBot: Selective Context Preservation

We built SaveBot on a simple principle: Preserve knowledge at the moment it's recognized as valuable, not after it's too late.

The User Experience

We designed SaveBot to be frictionless—preservation must take less than 10 seconds:

In Slack: /save thread PROJ-1234 "Architecture decision for service mesh"

Via Email: Forward to savebot@company.com with subject: [SAVE: PROJ-1234] Security review feedback

In Wiki: Add comment @savebot preserve to any page

Strategic Design Decisions

1. Storage Strategy

We evaluated multiple backends based on specific criteria:

2. Governance Model

Every preservation action requires:

  1. Business Justification: Link to ticket or brief explanation
  2. Audit Trail: Who saved, when, why—all logged
  3. Access Control: Inherits permissions from original source
  4. Quarterly Review: Purge outdated preservations

Critical Success Factors

1. Make It Frictionless

The moment someone thinks "this is important," they should be able to preserve it in under 10 seconds. Any friction kills adoption.

2. Preserve Context, Not Just Content

Save the entire discussion thread, not just the final decision. The "why" is often more valuable than the "what."

3. Require Meaningful Tags

Mandate connection to projects/tickets. Free-form preservation leads to unsearchable archives.

4. Regular Pruning

Not everything needs eternal preservation. Quarterly reviews keep the signal-to-noise ratio high.

5. Integrate with AI Workflows

Preserved knowledge is most valuable when it's accessible to both humans and AI agents through your MCP gateway.

Common Pitfalls and Solutions

Pitfall 1: Over-Preservation

Problem: Teams start saving everything "just in case"

Solution: Implement quotas (e.g., 10 preservations/user/week) and require justification tags

Pitfall 2: Permission Conflicts

Problem: Preserved content becomes accessible to those who shouldn't see it

Solution: Inherit and enforce original platform permissions; regular access audits

Pitfall 3: Stale Preservation

Problem: Preserved content becomes outdated but still appears in searches

Solution: Add "verified_until" dates and flag outdated content in search results
