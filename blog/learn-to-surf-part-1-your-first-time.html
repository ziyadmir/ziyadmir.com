Part 1: Your First Time - Getting Comfortable in the Water

July 2, 2025

Surfing Learning Beginner

Nobody tells you that your first surf session is mostly not about surfing. It is about meeting the ocean without pretending you are already comfortable there. The cold, the chop, the board that suddenly feels enormous, the leash tugging at your ankle, the mild panic when a wave breaks on your head — that is the actual curriculum. Around the San Francisco Bay the water is cold enough that the wetsuit is non-negotiable, and the first ten minutes are mostly your body filing complaints.

Day one has one job

Lower the fear response. That is it. Get in, float, fall, get tumbled, and discover that nothing magical happens when a wave knocks you over. You go under, you come up, the board is still attached to your leg. Comfort is the first skill because every later skill is built on top of it — a tense body cannot learn timing.

The hard part is being willing to be visibly bad at something as an adult. Most of us have quietly arranged our lives to avoid that feeling. The ocean does not care how athletic or smart you are, or what you do for a living. It rewards repetition and humility, and it punishes anyone trying to look competent ahead of schedule.

What actually helps

  • Pick a forgiving day and a forgiving spot. Small, slow, mushy waves are a feature, not an insult.
  • Ride a big stable soft-top, roughly the size of a door. The cool-looking short boards come much, much later.
  • Spend your attention on breathing, controlling the board, and getting back into position after each wipeout. Those are the reps that count.
  • Measure success in time spent in the water, not in moments where you looked like a surfer. There will be no such moments yet, and that is fine.

The first win is simple: leave the water wanting to come back. Everything else can wait.
