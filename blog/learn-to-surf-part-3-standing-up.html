Part 3: The Popup Paradox - Getting to Your Feet

July 5, 2025

Surfing Learning Beginner

By now you can catch white water, which means it is time for the popup — the move that takes you from lying on the board to standing on it. It looks simple on land and falls apart in the water. That is the paradox: you can understand the movement intellectually in ten seconds, then spend weeks discovering that understanding it is not the same as doing it while a wave is moving underneath you.

The window is small

By the time you have consciously thought through each limb — hands here, now the back foot — you are already late, and late means falling. The popup has to become automatic, which is another way of saying it has to live in your body rather than your head. Forget elegance for now. The goal is a transition from prone to feet that is stable enough for the ride to continue.

This is where adult learners get frustrated, and I suspect engineers especially. We are used to domains where understanding the thing is most of the work. Surfing exposes the gap between conceptual knowledge and embodied skill, and the only bridge across that gap is repetition. You cannot think your way to your feet.

What to practice

  • Drill the movement on land, but expect the water version to feel like a different move entirely. It is.
  • Hands near your ribs, chest up, eyes forward. Look where you are going, not down at your feet.
  • Accept ugly reps. A shaky popup that happens on time teaches you more than a pretty one that happens too late.

Standing up is not a single trick you learn once. It is a timing habit you earn through repetition — which is what the last part of this series is about.
