A few years ago, when I was putting in long hours at work and feeling the weight of my twenties, I started to notice a strange but persistent pattern. Young men roar through life like they're invincible, brimming with energy and bravado. Then, as we age, that energy shifts. Testosterone cools down and patience takes over, and suddenly the same risks that once seemed thrilling feel reckless.

The Allure of Youthful Charge

That youthful confidence isn't entirely a bad thing. When I was fresh out of university, I thought I knew everything. It looks naïve in hindsight, but it gave me the push to try things I might otherwise have avoided. Older colleagues watched me barrel ahead, shaking their heads, yet sometimes that bold hustle opened doors that would have remained shut.

The Slow Burn of Experience

As the years stack up, caution starts to feel more natural. I've met people with a decade or two more experience, and they know when to conserve energy for the moments that matter. In sports, in surfing, even in the office, they move with a precision that younger folks often overlook.

Finding the Balance

So which approach is right? Maybe both. Youthful audacity forces boundaries to shift, while seasoned patience keeps us from crashing and burning. I don't have the perfect formula, but I've come to believe that the trick is harnessing the best of each phase when the stakes call for it.
