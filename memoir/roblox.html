Roblox

2025–Present · San Francisco Bay Area

Career AI Moderation Current

This chapter is still being written, so I'll keep it short and resist the urge to narrate a story whose ending I don't know yet.

In 2025 I joined Roblox to work on moderation and governance systems — AI moderation for ads systems is the production proving ground. The pull was simple once I saw it: I had spent years teaching machines to read content, and Roblox has the hardest content I know of. Video holds still when you analyze it. Ad creative tries to be misread. Interactive experiences go further — much of what they contain only exists when someone plays them. And the audience skews young, which means the cost of a wrong read is measured in something heavier than revenue.

The work itself is the marriage of the two threads my career keeps braiding together: content understanding (can the system see what is actually there?) and governance (what is the AI allowed to do about it?). The next stretch is the part I am most excited about — putting agents inside the same sandbox the experiences run in, letting them explore and collect artifacts, and distilling what they find into experience understanding records the rest of the platform can build on. I've written that up properly on the work page and in my research notes.

Check back. The good anecdotes need a few more years before they're safe to tell.
