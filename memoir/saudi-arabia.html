Early Years in Saudi Arabia

1991-1995 · Jeddah
Childhood First Memories Family

I was born on September 30th, 1991 in Jeddah, Saudi Arabia. My parents are originally from Srinagar, Kashmir. My older brother was born two years before me. When I was 3.5 years old, we moved to Toronto, Canada. My second brother was born there.

Fragmented Memories

I don't remember much about living in Saudi Arabia, but I remember a few things. I remember going to a school, which I later learned was called the "Indian Embassy School", which I think is now called the "International Indian School Jeddah". I don't recall a single thing about classes, but I have one memory which is faded but for some reason sticks in my head.

My teacher was really nice and she used to help me find the bus — maybe I kept forgetting where I go to catch the bus, but I remember that I had a teacher who would hold my hand and then help me either figure out how to get to the bus stop or maybe walk me there. I don't know which one.

The other memory of school is that there was a talent show or a show and tell or something like that, and I remember either watching it or maybe being on stage. Life was really hazy around that time. We were young; I was 3.5 when we left, my older brother was 5.5, and my younger brother wasn't born yet — my mom must have been pregnant with him near the time we left.

My First Teacher

My dad worked at a place called Durrat-Al-Arus, which I remember as a sort of resort on the coast of Jeddah. We'd be in a room during the day with my mom, and she'd teach my older brother and I the alphabet, and how to read, and some simple math. I guess she was my first real teacher.

I remember us studying when it was daylight, partially because my mom wanted us to learn, partially because it was too hot to be outside, and partially because what else do you do as a woman in Jeddah with two young boys.

Evening Adventures

When the sun set, I remember slamming shut our books and running outside and down a long paved walkway to the pools. I recall the pools being right by the coast, and we could see an island somewhere in the distance in the Red Sea. These evening adventures were probably my earliest experiences of freedom and joy, creating a pattern of anticipation for what comes after the necessary work is done.
