Joining Uber in its Hypergrowth Phase

After my time at Opendoor, I joined Uber in 2016 during what many would consider its hypergrowth phase. The company was expanding globally at an unprecedented rate, and I was drawn to the challenge of helping scale a service that was critical to Uber's international presence.

Uber's rapid expansion into new countries created extraordinary internationalization challenges. The platform needed to support hundreds of millions of users across diverse languages and locales while maintaining a consistent, high-quality experience. This required not just translation of static content, but dynamic, context-aware translations that could handle the nuances of different languages and user types (riders, drivers, eaters, restaurants).

Technical Lead for Rosetta

I was brought in as the Technical Lead for Rosetta, Uber's internationalization (i18n) service. Rosetta was responsible for translating all user-facing content across Uber's ecosystem, enabling the platform to operate seamlessly across more than 70 countries and 30+ languages. What made this role particularly challenging was the scale at which Rosetta operated - it had the highest read throughput of all 2000+ microservices at Uber, handling over 1 million translation requests per second during peak traffic.

As Technical Lead, I was responsible for both the technical architecture and the team leadership aspects of Rosetta. My focus was on architecting a system that could deliver translations with sub-10ms response times while handling an ever-increasing volume of requests. This required innovative approaches to caching, distribution, and performance optimization.

Building a High-Performance Translation System

The technical challenges we faced were significant. We needed to design a system that could:

  • Handle 1M+ translation requests per second during peak traffic
  • Maintain sub-10ms response time for critical user flows
  • Support contextual translations for different user types
  • Manage a translation corpus that grew by thousands of strings weekly
  • Scale horizontally as Uber expanded to new regions

To address these challenges, we designed Rosetta as a high-performance, distributed translation system with multiple components:

  • Core Service - A Go-based API with highly optimized lookup paths
  • Translation Storage - Multi-tiered storage with hot path caching
  • Admin Portal - A React application for translation management
  • Client Libraries - SDKs for all Uber programming languages
  • Integration Layer - Connectors for translation vendor APIs

Performance Optimizations

One of my primary contributions was implementing a series of performance optimizations that allowed Rosetta to achieve its throughput and latency targets. These included:

  • Multi-level caching strategy (in-process, Redis, distributed cache)
  • Client-side caching with efficient invalidation mechanisms
  • Sharded storage for horizontal scaling
  • Custom serialization format for minimal memory footprint
  • Traffic throttling and circuit breakers for system stability

These optimizations allowed Rosetta to deliver translations with p99 latency under 10ms despite handling the highest request volume in Uber's ecosystem. This performance was critical for ensuring a seamless user experience across all of Uber's products and regions.

Integrating Machine Learning into Rosetta

Beyond the performance aspects, one of my most significant innovations was integrating machine learning into the translation workflow. Working closely with Uber's Michelangelo team (their ML platform), we developed systems that could:

  • Automatically suggest translations for new strings based on context
  • Predict the quality of translations for prioritizing human review
  • Rank translation alternatives based on context
  • Automatically detect and flag potential quality issues

This ML integration reduced translation costs by 35% while improving quality and reducing time-to-market for new languages. It also allowed Uber to respond more quickly to changing business needs and expand to new markets more efficiently.

Scaling Teams and Cross-Functional Leadership

Working at Uber's scale taught me invaluable lessons about technical leadership and cross-functional collaboration. As the technical lead for Rosetta, I was responsible for:

  • Managing a diverse team of engineers spanning backend, frontend, and ML disciplines
  • Coordinating with stakeholders across all Uber product lines (Rides, Eats, Freight, etc.)
  • Aligning with business priorities from different regions and markets
  • Balancing immediate needs with long-term architectural vision
  • Advocating for resources and prioritization within the organization

This experience helped me develop my leadership style, focusing on empowering team members, creating clear technical vision, and ensuring alignment with business objectives. I learned the importance of effective communication, particularly in a globally distributed organization with many competing priorities.

The Communications Platform

Beyond Rosetta, I also led the integration of ML into Uber's Communications Platform. This platform was responsible for all customer communications across channels like push notifications, SMS, and email. My team developed ML-driven systems that could:

  • Personalize message content and timing based on user behavior patterns
  • Optimize channel selection (push, SMS, email) using ML models
  • Predict engagement to minimize notification fatigue
  • A/B test message effectiveness at scale

This work resulted in a 50% increase in communication engagement and significant cost savings by reducing low-value notifications. It also improved the effectiveness of driver incentives through personalized messaging.

Technical Challenges and Growth

The technical challenges we faced at Uber forced me to develop new skills and approaches. Some of the most significant challenges included:

Scale & Performance

Handling the highest throughput at Uber required innovative approaches that I hadn't encountered before. We developed custom benchmark suites, implemented adaptive rate limiting, created specialized instrumentation for sub-millisecond latency tracking, and designed gradual rollout strategies for high-risk changes.

Global Deployment

Supporting global operations required addressing regional challenges like building multi-region deployment with data replication strategies, implementing region-specific fallback mechanisms, creating specialized handling for right-to-left languages, and developing support for complex pluralization rules across languages.

These challenges pushed me to become a better engineer and leader, developing a deeper understanding of distributed systems, performance optimization, and global-scale services.

Lessons Learned at Uber

My time at Uber taught me several valuable lessons that have shaped my approach to engineering leadership:

  • Performance at scale requires both systemic thinking and attention to detail - Understanding the end-to-end flow of data and optimizing critical paths is essential for high-scale systems.
  • Cross-functional collaboration is critical for success - Technical solutions alone aren't enough; alignment with business goals and coordination across teams is essential.
  • ML can transform traditional services - Integrating machine learning into core services can create step-function improvements in efficiency and effectiveness.
  • Global services require a nuanced approach - Different regions have different needs, constraints, and expectations that must be addressed in system design.
  • Building for resilience is as important as building for performance - Systems must be designed to degrade gracefully under stress and recover quickly from failures.
